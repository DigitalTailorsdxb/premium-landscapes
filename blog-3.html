Artificial Grass vs Natural Turf: Which is Right for Your UK Garden?

January 2025 • 6 min read

Choosing between artificial grass and natural turf? This comprehensive guide compares costs, maintenance, durability, and aesthetics to help you make the right decision for your UK garden.

Initial Cost Comparison

Natural Turf

£10-£18 per m²

Including preparation and installation

Artificial Grass

£48-£96 per m²

Including sub-base and installation

Maintenance Requirements

Natural Turf

  • Weekly mowing (spring-autumn)
  • Regular watering
  • Seasonal feeding & treatment
  • Weed control
  • Annual scarifying

Artificial Grass

  • Occasional brushing
  • Periodic rinsing
  • Remove debris
  • No mowing, watering, or feeding

Long-Term Cost Analysis

Over a 10-year period, natural turf maintenance costs (mowing, treatments, water) typically total £1,800-£3,600. Artificial grass requires minimal maintenance, making it more cost-effective long-term despite higher upfront costs.

Aesthetics & Feel

Modern artificial grass looks remarkably realistic, but natural turf still offers unmatched authenticity. Natural grass feels softer underfoot and has that distinctive fresh-cut aroma. However, artificial grass stays green year-round, even during dry UK summers or wet winters.

Durability & Lifespan

  • Natural Turf: Can last indefinitely with proper care, but needs regular renovation
  • Artificial Grass: 10-15 year lifespan before replacement needed

Best for UK Climate?

Natural turf thrives in the UK's temperate climate but struggles during droughts or heavy rain. Artificial grass performs consistently regardless of weather, making it ideal for high-traffic areas, shaded gardens, or families with children and pets.

Environmental Considerations

Natural turf is biodegradable, supports wildlife, and absorbs carbon. Artificial grass is made from plastic but saves water and eliminates pesticide/fertilizer use. Both have environmental trade-offs.
